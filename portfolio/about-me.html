Outside of Engineering

Outside of my engineering pursuits, I enjoy a variety of activities that keep me active and engaged. Sports like basketball and running have been passions of mine, and I enjoy spending time outdoors, whether it's hiking, exploring nature, or working on hands-on projects like woodworking. Growing up I developed a deep appreciation for craftsmanship through personal summer projects or construction jobs where I learned skills like framing and woodworking on various scales.

Learning is another passion of mine. I love school and constantly push myself to grow academically, taking upwards of 20 credits per semester to gain knowledge quickly and refine my time management skills. If I could come back to BYU-I and get a degree in another field like mechanical engineering, architecture, or any of the sciences in addition to my computer engineering degree, I would. Coming from a family where many of both my immediate family members and other relatives work in the education system, I was raised in an environment that values hard work, lifelong learning, and service to others. Their dedication to teaching and helping students succeed has instilled in me a love for learning and a deep respect for education. My enthusiasm for learning has caused me to be always looking for new skills to develop and opportunities to expand my understanding of the world and ability to improve it.

Additionally, my two years as a missionary in Zambia and Malawi helped me cultivate social skills while serving and teaching others. This role required me to focus deeply on the well-being of others and it strengthened my ability to communicate, collaborate, and care for people on a personal level.

These experiences, along with my hobbies and academic drive, have shaped me into a well-rounded individual who values education, learning, personal growth, and making a positive impact in the world.
