ISTE Standards for Educators 6: Facilitator

Educators facilitate learning with technology to support student achievement of the ISTE Standards for Students.

Foster Student Ownership of Learning

6.a. Foster a culture where students take ownership of their learning goals and outcomes in both independent and group settings.

Foster Classroom Management of Tech

6.b. Manage the use of technology and student learning strategies in digital platforms, virtual environments, hands-on makerspaces and in the field.

Teach Computational and Design Thinking

6.c. Create learning opportunities that challenge students to use a design process and computational thinking to innovate and solve problems.

Model and Nurture Creativity

6.d. Model and nurture creativity and creative expression to communicate ideas, knowledge or connections.

Artifact

Title: Preschool Learning Online

Families become a part of their child's early learning experience through virtual preschool experiences the whole family can enjoy. Four important features of online learning are shared in this article which educates parents and families in what they can expect from this unique experience including interactive Learning, flexible schedules, parent engagement, and a supportive learning community.

Building Community in a Virtual Pre-K Classroom

Foundational strategies of effective early childhood education that teachers used in the classroom work well for remote learning too.

Reflection

This article nurtures meaningful connection for families and supports the creative expression of ISTE Standard 6.d. Delivering age-appropriate, interactive lessons that foster curiosity, creativity, and a love for learning using a virtual platform supports ISTE Standard 6.b. Daily story time is an activity featured in the article, promising it to be the highlight of the preschool day. Using interactive technology such as Zoom will allows teachers to create this magical experience where students have fun, and develop early reading and language skills. I watched several videos of teachers delivering educational content through technology. I was impressed to see that even in virtual formats, students do more than watch the teacher on the screen. They interact, answer questions, and receive feedback.
