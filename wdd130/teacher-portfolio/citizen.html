ISTE Standards for Educators 3: Citizen

Educators inspire students to positively contribute to and responsibly participate in the digital world.

Create Positive Experiences

3.a. Create experiences for learners to make positive, socially responsible contributions and exhibit empathetic behavior online that build relationships and community.

Evaluate Resources for Credibility

3.b. Establish a learning culture that promotes curiosity and critical examination of online resources, and fosters digital literacy and media fluency.

Teach Safe, Legal, Ethical Practices

3.c. Mentor students in safe, legal and ethical practices with digital tools and the protection of intellectual rights and property.

Model Digital Privacy

3.d. Model and promote management of personal data and digital identity, and protect student data privacy.

Artifact

Title: Digital Citizenship and Social Media, a Doodly Presentation, by Rachel Young, 2023.

Teaching students about safety while using social media is crucial in today's digital age. Social media platforms offer various opportunities for communication, collaboration, and learning, but they also present potential risks. I created this video to provide students with information and guidelines to keep them safe while using platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, and Snapchat. While many students access these platforms on a daily basis, few of them actually understand the terms and conditions they agreed to when they created their account. In this video I highlight the "small print", the terms and conditions that are often overlooked.

Reflection

Preventative measures are often a focus of digital citizenship as indicated in the video presentation I created and by ISTE Standards 3.c and 3.d. I feel these performance indicators are important, but I have also learned that teaching resilience and how to proactively spread positivity in an online context is equally important as indicated by 3.a. As a parent of teenagers, I have learned how quickly a post, a video, a chat, or message can affect them - for better or worse. As a future educator I can expand digital citizenship to include resilient strategies for dealing with online negativity, hate speech, online conflicts, and other emotional challenges connected to a digital social life as well as information about how to create proactive online experiences that spread positivity and make our world a better place. Instilling all the ISTE performance indicators of digital citizenship helps students become responsible, ethical, and respectful online participants. This includes understanding the consequences of their actions, respecting others' rights, and contributing positively to the online community.
