ISTE Standards for Educators 1: Learner

Educators continually improve their practice by learning from and with others, and exploring proven and promising practices that leverage technology to boost student learning.

Set Professional Goals

1.a. Set professional learning goals to explore and apply pedagogical approaches made possible by technology and reflect on their effectiveness.

Participate in PLNs

1.b. Pursue professional interests by creating and actively participating in local and global learning networks.

Keep Current on Research

1.c. Stay current with research that supports improved student learning outcomes, including findings from the learning sciences.

Artifact

Title: A Framework for Thinking About Tech Integration, an article featured in Edutopia, written by P.E. France, 2020.

Edutopia, a trusted information source in the educational community, is working to transform education through the use of technology in the classroom. Whether you are an experienced and savvy tech user, or a novice like me, this article provides a framework that can guide your use of technology in the classroom. The author challenges educators to consider the purpose of technology and the importance of utilizing it in ways that will maximize learning while minimizing complexity. Further, the article reminds educators that technology should not distance students, rather it can be a powerful resource in connecting students, teachers, and families. I chose this artifact to serve as a guide for setting professional goals to learn about and use technology in the most effective ways.

A Framework for Thinking About Tech Integration

Apps and other digital tools should minimize complexity while prioritizing the authentic connections that are central to learning.

Reflection

We knew it was going to be a great day when our teacher wheeled a large cart into our classroom holding a television and a VCR player! We loved any opportunity to escape the regular lecture and routine of the classroom and learn from a machine that brought information to life and kept our attention. In addition to the occasional informational video, we could earn time in the “book nook” and enjoy a read-along-book with a cassette player and headphones. Thirty years ago this is how I would have defined instructional technology in the classroom and it was often used as a reward for good work or behavior in the classroom. Thankfully, Instructional technology today goes far beyond what I experienced and can include an array of digital devices, software applications for planning, learning, progress monitoring, and even assessing. I have learned that instructional/educational technology is about integrating various tools, techniques, and resources to improve instruction and enhance learning. It is not simply using technology in the classroom but discovering ways to incorporate it as a way to increase my capacity and effectiveness as an educator. With my newfound knowledge, and guidance from ISTE Standard 1.a, I will set goals to explore and apply technology in my future classroom based on this framework.
